AI in Education Educational Data Mining Learning Analytics Large-Scale Assessments

My research lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data science, and education. I investigate how data-driven methods can deepen our understanding of learning, support educational decision-making, and contribute to the development of more effective educational systems.

Drawing on approaches from educational data mining, learning analytics, and machine learning, I study learning processes and educational outcomes using large-scale assessment data. My work focuses on developing rigorous, interpretable, and reproducible methods for analyzing complex educational datasets and generating evidence that can inform both research and practice.

I am particularly interested in international large-scale assessments, artificial intelligence in education, and the use of large language models to support educational research. In parallel, I explore how machine learning and artificial intelligence can be taught effectively, contributing to research on machine learning education.

Through my research, I aim to bridge advances in artificial intelligence with meaningful educational challenges, creating methods and tools that support evidence-based educational research and innovation.

  • Educational Data Mining
    The application of machine learning and statistical modeling to data generated within educational systems, with the goal of uncovering patterns that inform instruction, policy, and system design. EDM treats educational datasets as complex, hierarchically structured sources of evidence requiring methodologically appropriate analytic approaches.
  • Learning Analytics
    The measurement, collection, and analysis of data about learners in order to understand and optimize learning and the environments in which it occurs. Learning analytics bridges educational research and data science, connecting findings from large-scale assessment programmes to actionable evidence for researchers and practitioners.
  • LLMs for Evidence Synthesis
    The use of large language models with schema-constrained output validation to extract structured evidence from bodies of research literature at scale. This approach enables systematic, auditable synthesis of findings across thousands of studies, producing queryable knowledge bases that support evidence-informed research.
  • International Large-Scale Assessments
    Cross-national educational surveys — including PISA, TIMSS, PIRLS, TALIS, PIAAC, ICILS, and ICCS — that generate rich comparative evidence about student achievement, educator practices, and system-level factors. Valid secondary analysis of ILSA data requires careful attention to plausible values, replicate weights, and complex sampling designs.
  • Machine Learning Education
    The design, implementation, and evaluation of curricula and instructional approaches that develop machine learning competencies in students who are not computer science majors. This includes pre-service mathematics teachers, education faculty, and other disciplinary contexts where ML is becoming methodologically relevant.
  • Explainable AI in Education
    The use of model interpretability methods — including SHAP and LIME — to make predictive models applied to educational data transparent and actionable for practitioners who must make decisions on the basis of model outputs. Interpretability is understood here as a functional requirement rather than a technical nicety.
